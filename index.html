Food

In Argentina, Asado is a fundamental part of the culinary culture, and meals are often prolonged social events. In Japan, sushi and ramen are iconic dishes, and meals can be more formal and focused on aesthetic presentation.

Table manners

In Argentina, it's common to talk and laugh loudly during group meals. In Japan, silence and moderation while eating are valued.

Outfit

In Japan, clothing tends to be more conservative and formal. In the workplace, employees are expected to wear formal suits in most places. They have the kimono, which is still worn on special occasions and ceremonies.

Hierarchy

In Argentina, hierarchy can be less rigid, and people tend to treat their superiors with greater familiarity. In Japan, respect for hierarchy is essential, and specific titles and forms of language are used to address people of different statuses.

Time and punctuality

In Argentina, punctuality can be more flexible, and meetings might start with a certain delay. In Japan, punctuality is extremely important, and arriving on time is considered a sign of respect.
